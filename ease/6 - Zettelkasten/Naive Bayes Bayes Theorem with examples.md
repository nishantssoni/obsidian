- We are asking that if an item is chosen at random from the total output and it's found to be defective(even B), what is the probability that it was produced by a third machine?
- 0.024 means that 2.4% of all products are defective.
- The evidence P(B) in Bayes' theorem is the total probability of B, considering all possible ways it can happen.
- Posterior (P(A∣B): The updated probability of A given that B has occurred.
- Likelihood (P(B∣A)): The probability of B occurring if A is true.
- Prior (P(A)): The initial probability of A, before considering B.
