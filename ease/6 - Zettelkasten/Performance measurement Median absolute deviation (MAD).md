- $SS_{res} = \sum(e_{i}^{2})$
- if one of the errors is very large, then R2 is highly impacted, it is not robust to outliers
- Median Absolute Deviation is a robust metric of standard deviation.
- MAD = Median ($|e_{i} - Median(e_{i}'s)|$)
- If we don’t have outliers then we can use mean and std-dev and if we have outliers which impact our outputs then we use medium and MAD instead of mean and std-dev, because median is just like mean and MAD is just like std-dev.
- first we have to calculate the median of error and then we have to check MAD if MAD and median are small of then model is good else it is bad.
